Fighting air pollution

One of the largest problems that thriving societies are facing is the damage that they are creating to the global environment by using factories and vehicles that poisons the air we all breath on a daily basis. Combustion cars are constantly polluting the air making life harder for those that live with a permanent stream of toxic air. The result of a lifetime of poisonous air consumption are clear: asthma, bronchitis, lung cancer, cardiovascular problems and a threat to pregnant and newborns.

Air pollution causes a reduction of a 70% of visible distance which is dangerous for those that use vehicles on a regular basis. Consequently, all cars have to use filters to protect motors and passengers from toxic air particles. If we don't actively work on this problem right now, future generations will suffer from genetic diseases such as diabetes and a decline in human intelligence on a massive global scale.

Helping the world with trees

Plants as we all know it, are the main natural filters that have been converting generating breathable air for mammals for hundreds of millions of years. They are great at what they do with minimal maintainance. Technology will continue producing more environmentally harmful devices since it's a normal step in human evolution, therefore we'll balance that effect with more and more green sources of pure air.
